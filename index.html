Mala Fries Recipe with Szechuan Peppercorns

This recipe was inspired during a recent late night chat with my friend living in Thailand. She sent me a photo (below) of the mala fries she just ordered at a really popular Thai joint. I tried going to bed, but mind couldn't forget about what I had saw, what my soul was yearning for. My stomach growled, and I knew my mind, body, and spirit, just had to have a taste of that magical mala I heard so much about.




    So, without further ado, let's get into how to make these tasty, tongue-numbing fries (or chips as they are called everywhere else in the world!).

    The real key is to toast up the szechuan peppercorns first for a more flavourful toasty aroma. Then, bring out the pepper/coffee grinder (mine was electric) and grind the peppercorns into a fine powder. After that, you can mix up your usual favourite suspects.

  • More
    • For me, I chose to go with all purpose seasoning, parpika, some garlic powder, onion powder, chilli powder, and hot chili powder. I pre-mixed it in a small bowl, and then sprinkled it on, and tossed the fries in a stainless steel mixing bowl (Thanks to my line-cook days at Earls Restaurant). I find this method to produce the best results (i.e. more powder coverage = more flava flaveeeee!!)). It's a bit salty, so it pairs very nicely with a cold brewski. Hope you get a chance to try out this quick and easy recipe. Next time, I think I'm going to try adding a shit ton more paprika for that beautiful red colour. If you do, let me know how you liked it, and if you tried putting your own spin on my spin of the not yet, but will be, world-famous Mala Fries! Enjoy!
